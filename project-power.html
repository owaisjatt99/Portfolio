Diploma of Associate Engineering (Final Year Project)

Electrical Generation and Distribution System

Designing and implementing a small-scale integrated electrical network, from hydel power generation to multi-tier voltage transformation and distribution.

Hydel Power Generation

Generating electrical power by coupling a single-phase AC motor to a small water turbine to convert kinetic energy into electrical energy.

  • AC generation and synchronization.
  • Mechanical-electrical coupling.
  • Basic hydel system principles.

Transformer Fabrication

Designing and manually winding step-up and step-down transformers to meet specific distribution requirements.

  • Transformer winding and calculations.
  • Magnetic core assembly.
  • Hands-on electrical fabrication.

Power Distribution System

Implementing an overhead line system to distribute AC power with proper voltage regulation and safety precautions.

  • AC power distribution networks.
  • Overhead line installation.
  • Safety protocols in power systems.

AC to DC Conversion

Using full-wave bridge rectifier circuits and filtering to supply stable DC power to LED loads and electronics.

  • Power electronics and rectification.
  • Rectifier circuit design and filtering.
  • LED powering and DC loads.

Load Connection & Testing

Connecting AC/DC loads to verify performance, monitoring voltage, current, and overall system stability.

  • Electrical testing and monitoring.
  • System integration and validation.
  • Troubleshooting and performance optimization.

Project Outcome

  • Successfully generated, transformed, and distributed electrical power using a small-scale hydel system.
  • Developed hands-on skills in transformer fabrication, AC/DC conversion, and distribution network setup.
  • Demonstrated integration of theoretical knowledge with practical application in electrical engineering.
